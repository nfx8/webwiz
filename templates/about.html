about

This is a demo inspired by d3.js and a series of talks from Ted Nelson called Computers for Cynics. He points out that what are called technologies are mostly packages and conventions and that software often simulates paper. Visualizations often display graphics as pure outputs. Here with the interactive chart based on the d3.js library the user can select parts of the chart and see results. For instance linear regression can be misleading based metrics such as R^2 ignore part of the structure. I believe advanced mathematical techniques such as kernels are actually much easier to grasp with the use of interactive charts.
